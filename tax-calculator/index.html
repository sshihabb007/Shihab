1. Salary Exemption

For salaried individuals, a portion of the income is completely tax-exempt. According to the new laws, the exemption limit is 1/3 of the total salary income or 450,000 BDT, whichever is lower.

2. Tax-Free Thresholds

  • General (Male): 350,000 BDT (Calculated as 375,000 BDT in some projected cases)
  • Women & Senior Citizens (65+): 400,000 BDT
  • Physically Challenged: 475,000 BDT
  • War-Wounded Freedom Fighters: 500,000 BDT
  • Note: Parents/Legal Guardians of physically challenged individuals get an additional 50,000 BDT tax-free limit.

3. Tax Slabs (General)

After deducting the tax-free limit, the remaining taxable income is taxed according to the following progressive slabs:

Taxable Income Slab Tax Rate
Next 100,000 BDT 5%
Next 400,000 BDT 10%
Next 500,000 BDT 15%
Next 500,000 BDT 20%
Remaining Balance 25% (Max)

4. Investment Rebate

Taxpayers can claim a rebate on eligible investments (e.g., DPS, Life Insurance, Sanchaypatra). The allowable rebate is the lowest of the following three amounts:

  • 3% of Total Taxable Income
  • 15% of Actual Eligible Investment
  • 1,000,000 BDT (Maximum limit)

5. Surcharge (Wealth Tax)

Surcharge applies if your Net Wealth exceeds certain thresholds or if specific ownership conditions are met:

  • Up to 4 Crore: Nil (Unless you own a motor car or house property in a city corporation, which triggers a minimum 10% surcharge).
  • 4 Crore to 10 Crore: 10% of Net Tax
  • 10 Crore to 20 Crore: 20% of Net Tax
  • 20 Crore to 50 Crore: 30% of Net Tax
  • Above 50 Crore: 35% of Net Tax

6. Minimum Tax

If your taxable income exceeds the tax-free limit, you must pay at least the minimum tax, which depends on your location:

  • Dhaka & Chattogram City Corporation: 5,000 BDT
  • Other City Corporations: 4,000 BDT
  • Outside City Corporations: 3,000 BDT
